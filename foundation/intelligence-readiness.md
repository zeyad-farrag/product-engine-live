# Intelligence Readiness Assessment

This assessment evaluates whether the foundation provides sufficient context for each Product Engine capability to operate effectively. Readiness is rated as "ready" (can run with confidence), "partial" (can run but with lower confidence or gaps), or "not ready" (critical information missing).

| Capability | Data Available | Readiness | Gaps |
|---|---|---|---|
| Persona Definition (pe-persona-definition) | Source market client counts by country (866K clients across 243 countries), language preferences (10 languages), customer segmentation by tier and purpose, demographic patterns by market, brand-to-segment mapping, booking lead times by market | **Ready** | Internal NPS/satisfaction scores beyond TripAdvisor not available in database; detailed demographic breakdowns (age, income) are limited in client table |
| Competitor Benchmarking (pe-competitor-benchmarking) | 10+ named competitors with positioning analysis, competitive dynamics by market segment and source market, indirect competitor mapping (OTAs, DIY platforms), competitive intelligence gap acknowledged | **Ready** | No automated competitor price monitoring; competitor data is qualitative (from foundation research), not quantitative from systematic collection |
| Demand Signal Mining (pe-demand-signal-mining) | Direct MySQL access to 849K requests with source attribution, 866K clients with country_id, 520K service orders, 82K operation files, 303K enquiries. Request table has 100+ columns including dates, pricing, status lifecycle, UTM tracking, device type, language. Data spans 2010-present with density from 2015+ | **Ready** | Web analytics (Google Analytics) not accessible via database; social media engagement metrics in external platforms; some varchar fields (budget, flexible_date) have inconsistent formats |
| Product Health Check (pe-product-health-check) | 17K packages in tr_packages linked to 8 destinations, request-to-operation conversion trackable, revenue data in acc_srv_orders (520K records), request status lifecycle for funnel analysis, seasonal pricing data, multi-brand performance comparison possible via source_id | **Ready** | Detailed supplier cost data may be in separate system (margin calculation limited); customer satisfaction scores not systematically captured in database |
| Gap Analysis (pe-gap-analysis) | Complete product catalog structure (17K packages × 8 destinations × 4 tiers × 9 product types), customer segmentation model, competitive positioning data, source market preferences by tier and purpose, pricing benchmarks | **Ready** | Competitor product-level detail (specific itineraries, pricing) not systematically collected; customer feedback themes not structured in database |

| Portfolio Health (pe-portfolio-health) | All prerequisite data types available — product catalog, market data, competitive landscape, demand signals accessible, pricing data, organizational context | **Partial** | Most valuable after multiple health checks and demand signal analyses have been run and stored as artifacts; currently no artifacts exist — this capability improves as the artifact repository grows |
| Cross-Initiative Patterns (pe-cross-initiative-patterns) | Foundation complete, intelligence store structure ready | **Partial** | This is a Layer 3 capability that reads across completed initiatives. No initiatives have been run yet. Becomes ready after 3+ initiatives are completed and persisted to the repository |

## Summary

Ten of thirteen capabilities are rated **Ready** — the foundation provides sufficient context and data access for them to operate with confidence. Two Layer 3 intelligence capabilities (Portfolio Health, Cross-Initiative Patterns) are rated **Partial** because they depend on accumulated artifacts from completed initiatives rather than foundation data alone. These will become fully ready as the system is used and artifacts accumulate.

The single strongest data asset is the direct MySQL connection to the production database with 849K requests, 866K clients, and 520K service orders. This gives demand signal mining, product health checks, and market intelligence capabilities unusually rich quantitative grounding.

The most significant gap across all capabilities is the absence of systematic competitor data collection. The foundation provides qualitative competitive intelligence, but quantitative benchmarking (competitor prices, product catalogs, review scores) will need to be gathered at runtime by each capability.
