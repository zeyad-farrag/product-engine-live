IDENTITY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Demographics:     Age 40–65. Upper-middle to high income (household €80K–€150K+).
                  Couples or solo. Often Paris, Lyon, Bordeaux, or university cities.
                  Retired professionals, academics, senior executives, teachers,
                  doctors. Post-children household or empty-nesters.
Traveler type:    Cultural expert / intellectual explorer. Has likely traveled to
                  Greece, Italy, Jordan, Turkey, and Petra. Egypt is a pilgrimage,
                  not a bucket-list tick. May have visited 2–3 times already.
Travel frequency: 2–3 international trips per year. One "serious" cultural
                  destination per year. Reads before traveling; re-reads after.

MOTIVATIONS & NEEDS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Primary motivation:    Deep intellectual engagement with ancient Egyptian
                       civilization — temples, tombs, hieroglyphic inscriptions,
                       mythology, funerary rites. France's connection to Egyptology
                       via Napoleon's 1798 expedition and Jean-François Champollion's
                       decipherment of hieroglyphs (1822) creates a uniquely French
                       sense of cultural ownership over this history. [Art Institute of
                       Chicago: Egyptomania in France]
Secondary motivations: Nile cruise as the classic French romantic ideal; access to
                       restricted or off-the-beaten-path sites (Abydos, Deir
                       el-Medina, Sehel Island inscriptions); witnessing French
                       archaeological missions in situ (48 active missions cited by
                       French Ambassador Chevalier, Dec 2024). [Daily News Egypt]
Emotional drivers:     Intellectual mastery and discovery. The satisfaction of
                       understanding what others merely photograph. Cultural pride —
                       "C'est nous qui avons déchiffré les hiéroglyphes." Awe at scale
                       and antiquity. A deep nostalgia for the Romantic-era travel
                       narratives of Flaubert, Champollion, and Du Camp.
Unmet needs:           Guides who match their knowledge level — most operators offer
                       guides who recite scripts. This traveler needs an expert
                       Egyptologist, not a tour leader. Wants access to sites before
                       the crowds (early morning exclusives), scholarly commentary,
                       and French-language resources. Frustrated by itineraries that
                       compress Karnak into 90 minutes.

DECISION JOURNEY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Trigger:          A Louvre exhibition, a documentary (e.g., ARTE), a book, a
                  French archaeological announcement, or the Grand Egyptian Museum
                  opening. Retirement or a milestone anniversary also triggers.
Research phase:   Long and meticulous. 3–6 months minimum. Reads operator
                  websites in depth — specifically guide credentials and itinerary
                  detail. Cross-references with Lonely Planet Egypte, specialized
                  French travel books (e.g., Guides Bleus Égypte), French Egyptology
                  forums, and TripAdvisor in French. Will email operators with
                  specific questions about guide qualifications.
Comparison:       Experience-first. Will compare itinerary depth, guide expertise,
                  and site access before comparing price. Willing to pay a premium for
                  the right guide. Will reject a cheaper operator if the guide
                  biography is absent or unconvincing.
Decision factors: 1. Guide's Egyptological credentials (academic or certified)
                  2. French-language service throughout
                  3. Itinerary access to lesser-visited sites (not just Giza/Luxor)
                  4. Accommodation quality (4–5 star or historic Nile boat)
                  5. Group size (private or max 8–10 pax)
                  6. Operator reputation and longevity
                  7. Price (important, but not primary)
Booking channel:  memphistours.fr (dominant; 1,586 bookings via French/Egypt channel
                  at avg €4,423 — internal data). Also Egypte Voyages sub-channel
                  (37 bookings at avg €6,742 — highest-value channel). Direct email
                  or phone with a specialist consultant.
Lead time:        84 days average (internal data), but this persona typically plans
                  further ahead — 3–6 months. Luxury tier bookings likely extend to
                  90–150 days.

PRODUCT EXPECTATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Accommodation:  5-star Nile-view hotels in Cairo and Luxor (Sofitel Winter Palace,
                Marriott Mena House near Pyramids). Classic or luxury Nile cruise
                vessel (minimum 5-star MS); preference for smaller boutique ships
                or dahabiya for authenticity.
Activities:     Full temple circuits: Karnak, Luxor, Hatshepsut, Valley of the Kings
                and Queens, Abydos (Seti I temple — the crown jewel for Egyptophiles),
                Dendera, Edfu, Kom Ombo, Abu Simbel, Saqqara, Memphis. Museum visits
                with curatorial depth — Grand Egyptian Museum guided by a specialist.
                Optional: Coptic Cairo, Alexandrian Greco-Roman sites.
Duration:       10–14 days. Will not accept a rushed 7-day itinerary.
Group model:    Private tour for 2. Occasionally a private group of 4–8 with friends
                who share the same passion. Never a coach group.
Service level:  Full concierge. Egyptologist guide (French-speaking, ideally
                with university or IFAO credentials). Pre-trip reading list supplied.
                Private vehicles. VIP/early access to sites where available.
Must-haves:     - Francophone Egyptologist guide (non-negotiable)
                - Nile cruise component
                - Abydos and/or lesser-known Upper Egypt sites
                - Private transport
                - Pre-trip documentation (itinerary, guide bio, site notes in French)
Deal-breakers:  - Generic coach tour
                - English-only guides
                - Itinerary limited to "the big three" (Giza, Luxor, Aswan only)
                - Poor guide credentials
                - Mass-tourism cruise ships with 200+ passengers

PRICE & VALUE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Budget range:            €2,500–€6,000+ per person for a 10–14 day private tour.
                         Internal data confirms avg booking value $4,275 (all France),
                         with ultra-luxury tier (926 bookings) averaging $9,797 and
                         luxury tier (502 bookings) averaging $3,752. Couple bookings
                         average $6,669 (internal data — only 15 recorded couples,
                         likely undercounted due to xl_group classification).
Value perception:        "Best experience per euro" — not cheapest, not most
                         ostentatious. Value = depth of intellectual access +
                         guide quality + site exclusivity. Will reject a luxury product
                         that lacks scholarly credibility.
Price anchor:            Compares to other cultural specialist operators (Voyageurs
                         du Monde, Clio Voyages, Terres d'Aventure). Aware of DIY
                         cost but values curation and access they cannot arrange alone.
Willingness to upgrade:  For a certified Egyptologist guide, early-site access,
                         a dahabiya (traditional Nile sailing vessel), or exclusive
                         dinner at a heritage site.

CONTENT & CHANNEL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Content preferences:  Long-form. Detailed itinerary PDFs, guide biographies with
                      credentials, scholarly blog posts about Egyptian history,
                      site-specific video walkthroughs. ARTE documentaries. French
                      Egyptology magazine content (Archéologia, Les Cahiers de
                      Science et Vie). NOT short-form social content.
Trusted sources:      French specialist operators (Clio Voyages benchmark), French
                      archaeological mission announcements (CNRS, IFAO Cairo),
                      TripAdvisor reviews in French from verified cultural travelers,
                      word-of-mouth among friends/alumni networks. The Musée du
                      Louvre's Egypt department website.
Social platforms:     Facebook (69% of French travelers — internal research data),
                      YouTube (documentaries and site walkthroughs), LinkedIn for
                      professional communities. Instagram secondary — follows
                      Egyptology accounts, not travel influencers.
Language:             French exclusively. Will immediately discount an operator
                      whose French-language materials are visibly translated/poor.
Tone that resonates:  Intellectual and authoritative — with warmth. Reference
                      Champollion, Mariette, Maspero. Use hieroglyphic terms
                      correctly. Speak to them as equals. Not "discover the
                      wonders of Egypt" — but "explore the Ramesside program at
                      Abydos with our resident Egyptologist."
