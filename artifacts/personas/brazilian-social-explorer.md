IDENTITY

Demographics:     Ages 25–40; middle to upper-middle class urban professional
                  (São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, Belo Horizonte); single or in early
                  relationship; moderate disposable income; employed in service,

Traveler type:    Social experience collector; status-signaling traveler; content
                  creator by instinct.
Travel frequency: 1–2 international trips per year; first or second trip to the
                  Middle East/North Africa corridor.

MOTIVATIONS & NEEDS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Primary motivation:    Creating shareable, "bucket-list" content — the Pyramids,
                       Nile sunset, Abu Simbel — that signals cultural daring
                       to peers on Instagram and TikTok.
Secondary motivations: Group bonding experience; novelty (Egypt is exotic enough
                       to be impressive, structured enough to feel safe); value
                       for money on a long-haul flight investment.
Emotional drivers:     Social validation, FOMO avoidance, collective joy, and
                       pride in being the person who organised the trip.
Unmet needs:           Portuguese-language support throughout the journey;
                       itineraries flexible enough for spontaneous detours or
                       long café stops; WhatsApp-first customer service; clear,
                       group-friendly pricing breakdowns.

DECISION JOURNEY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Trigger:          A Brazilian travel influencer's Instagram reel or YouTube vlog
                  featuring the Pyramids or a Nile cruise; a friend returns from
                  Egypt with compelling social content; a WhatsApp group chat
                  where someone proposes "let's go to Egypt together."
Research phase:   Heavy Instagram and YouTube consumption (2–4 weeks); searches
                  in Portuguese; checks Egyptian Sidekick website and Google
                  reviews; WhatsApp group debate with friends about dates
                  and budget; minimal comparison of operators by brand—
                  trusts whoever has the best content and reviews.
Comparison:       Price-sensitive but not price-first; values perceived fairness
                  ("good deal for the group"); compares all-in package cost
                  per person; less likely to build DIY itinerary — prefers
                  structured packages that reduce coordination effort.
Decision factors: 1. Per-person price visibility  2. Portuguese language
                  3. Group accommodation options  4. Itinerary variety
                  (beach + culture mix)  5. Social proof / reviews
Booking channel:  Egyptian Sidekick (Brazilian Site / Egypt) — dominant;
                  completes booking via desktop after WhatsApp consultation.
Lead time:        ~90–100 days; compressed when trip is pegged to a local
                  holiday cluster (Carnival, June festivals, Semana Santa).

PRODUCT EXPECTATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Accommodation:  3–4 star hotels; comfortable but not extravagant; shared
                multi-room configurations for the group.
Activities:     Pyramids of Giza (mandatory), Nile cruise, Luxor temples,
                Khan el-Khalili market, Cairo food experiences; optional
                desert safari add-on.
Duration:       8–12 days; often combined with a Dubai or Turkey stopover.
Group model:    Private group tour (not mixed with strangers); flexible
                daily schedule preferred.
Service level:  Guided with a Portuguese-speaking or bilingual local guide;
                WhatsApp concierge throughout the trip.
Must-haves:     Full Portuguese communication (pre-trip and on-ground);
                photogenic stops and "Instagram moments" built into itinerary;
                group discounts or family/friends pricing.
Deal-breakers:  English-only communication; rigid timetables with no
                breathing room; poor photo opportunities; hidden fees
                discovered after booking.

PRICE & VALUE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Budget range:            $900–$1,500 per person all-in (classic tier);
                         total group booking $4,500–$9,000.
Value perception:        "Best experience per real spent." Wants to feel they
                         got more than they paid for; social validation of the
                         choice confirms value post-trip.
Price anchor:            Compares against a domestic resort trip to Natal or
                         Fernando de Noronha, which can cost $800–$1,200 per
                         person — Egypt needs to feel competitively priced.
Willingness to upgrade:  Will add a single Nile cruise upgrade or a desert camp
                         night if presented as an iconic group memory; rarely
                         upgrades accommodation; may add camel ride, hot air
                         balloon, or sound-and-light show.

Social platforms:     Instagram (primary discovery), YouTube (research),
                      TikTok (inspiration), WhatsApp (group coordination

Language:             Brazilian Portuguese exclusively; English content is

Tone that resonates:  Warm, inclusive, adventurous, slightly informal;
