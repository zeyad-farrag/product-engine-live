IDENTITY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Demographics:     Ages 40–60; household income $60,000–$150,000 USD
                  (significant purchasing power in local currency context);
                  business owner, senior professional, or entrepreneur; resides
                  in Mexico City, Buenos Aires, Bogotá, Guadalajara, Medellín;
                  travels in organized groups of 6–10 organized by a single
                  "trip leader" — often a woman in her 40s–50s who is the
                  social hub of a professional or family-adjacent circle.
Traveler type:    Aspirational group organizer; culturally curious but
                  comfort-oriented; values prestige and meaningful experience
                  over extreme adventure; has done the Caribbean and Europe;
                  Egypt/Jordan/Turkey represents the next-level long-haul
                  milestone. Prior international travel experience typical.
Travel frequency: 1 major international group trip per year plus 1–2
                  regional trips; Egypt or Jordan positions as a "once in a
                  decade" landmark journey. Will combine destinations across
                  a 2–3 week itinerary to maximize distance traveled.

MOTIVATIONS & NEEDS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Primary motivation:    Multi-country prestige journey — Egypt + Jordan (Petra)
                       + Turkey (Istanbul, Cappadocia) as the defining LATAM
                       "grand tour" of the Middle East and ancient civilizations.
                       The itinerary signals sophistication and worldliness
                       within a social circle. Completing the Pyramids, Petra,
                       and Cappadocia in one trip is a powerful status narrative.
Secondary motivations: Nile cruise as romantic and logistically seamless
                       accommodation solution; private or small-group guided
                       experiences that avoid the "mass tourism" feeling;
                       high-quality photography moments for social sharing on
                       Instagram and family WhatsApp groups. Culinary discovery
                       (Egyptian mezze, Jordanian mansaf, Turkish breakfast) is
                       a meaningful secondary draw for LATAM travelers, who bring
                       strong food culture to their travel expectations.
Emotional drivers:     Achievement ("hice el viaje soñado"), group pride, and
                       the desire to share an extraordinary experience with
                       close friends or family. The trip organizer's reputation
                       is on the line — quality and logistics must be flawless.
                       Egypt's Pharaonic scale produces genuine awe; Jordan's
                       rose-red canyon of Petra delivers the iconic moment.
Unmet needs:           Spanish-language guides of genuine Egyptology depth (not
                       just Spanish-speaking local guides); a single operator
                       who can coordinate Egypt + Jordan + Turkey seamlessly;
                       clear group payment logistics (one master invoice +
                       per-person installment options); pre-trip cultural
                       briefing materials in Spanish to prepare the group.

DECISION JOURNEY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Trigger:          A friend group's Instagram post from Wadi Rum or the Nile;
                  a luxury travel influencer's YouTube tour of Egypt in Spanish;
                  the organizer's personal bucket-list aspiration, often
                  crystallized at a life milestone (50th birthday, anniversary,
                  retirement). Vamos Viajando brand positioning directly targets
                  this "organized LATAM group" archetype.
Research phase:   2–4 months; the organizer does substantial research via
                  Spanish-language travel blogs, YouTube channels covering
                  Middle East travel, and Instagram; then contacts 2–3 operators
                  for group quotes. The group is presented with a shortlist —
                  not an open-ended choice. Argentina avg lead time: 111.5 days;
                  Mexico: 86.8 days; Colombia: 83.0 days — Argentina books
                  furthest ahead, indicating greater planning deliberateness.
Comparison:       Evaluates on quality of Spanish communication, multi-country
                  coordination capability, guide credentials, and total value.
                  Will compare Vamos Viajando against Spanish-market operators
                  (Europamundo, TUI Latinoamérica) and direct regional agencies.
                  Egypt Tours Plus / Spanish converts at high value: Mexico $6,093,
                  Colombia $7,208, Argentina $5,706 — confirming premium appetite
                  when quality proposition is clear.
Decision factors: 1. Spanish-speaking guide quality and cultural authority
                  2. Multi-country itinerary (Egypt + Jordan is minimum; Turkey
                     extension highly desired for groups willing to extend)
                  3. Nile cruise quality (4★+ motor vessel or Dahabiya)
                  4. Group logistics: single-operator coordination, rooming
                     flexibility, joint airport transfer, one invoice
                  5. Price-per-person transparency and payment plan options
                  6. Peer recommendation from a trusted source who has traveled
                     with the same operator ("¿con quién fueron?")
Booking channel:  Spanish Site / Egypt (Argentina: 1,204 bookings, avg $3,164;
                  Mexico: 707 bookings, avg $4,052; Colombia: 441 bookings,
                  avg $4,213); Vamos Viajando / Egypt (Argentina: 39 bookings,
                  avg $5,087; Mexico: 39 bookings, avg $6,333; Colombia: 32
                  bookings, avg $5,155) — Vamos Viajando is the premium LATAM
                  channel; Spain Multi-country Spanish also captures this
                  segment (Mexico: 115 bookings, avg $5,132; Colombia: 64
                  bookings, avg $5,417); Cruceros Nilo (Argentina: 23 avg
                  $8,090; Mexico: 41 avg $8,227) confirms Nile cruise premium.

PRODUCT EXPECTATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Accommodation:  4–5★ hotels in Cairo (Marriott, Kempinski, Four Seasons Nile
                City preferred); 4★ motor vessel Nile cruise or Dahabiya
                for smaller groups; 5★ hotels in Amman or Aqaba for Jordan
                extension; Cave hotel or boutique property in Cappadocia for
                Turkey. Expects reliable air-conditioning, room size adequate
                for couples (king bed), quality breakfast included, and concierge
                capable of handling group requests. Does not want dormitory or
                hostel configurations in any part of the trip.
Activities:     Egypt: Pyramids + Sphinx private sunrise visit; Egyptian Museum
                or Grand Egyptian Museum; full Luxor–Aswan Nile cruise; Abu
                Simbel flight; optional felucca and Nubian village. Jordan:
                Petra full-day with private guide; Wadi Rum jeep safari +
                overnight camp; Dead Sea float (essential LATAM checkbox).
                Turkey extension: Istanbul Bosphorus cruise, Grand Bazaar,
                Cappadocia hot air balloon, Pamukkale. All activities private
                or small-group; no mixing with random coach tours.
Duration:       12–21 days for multi-country; Egypt only: 8–12 days.
                Mexico avg group size 5.91, Argentina 5.57, Colombia 4.85 —
                groups of 5–6 per booking, potentially multiple bookings
                from the same trip.
Group model:    Premium organized group (6–10 pax from a social circle);
                xl_group dominates all three LATAM markets (Mexico: 2,295
                bookings, avg $4,711; Argentina: 2,722, avg $3,585; Colombia:
                1,265, avg $4,678). Vamos Viajando brand is the dedicated
                vehicle for this configuration.
Service level:  Fully guided and escorted; expects a single Spanish-speaking
                guide-escort for the entire trip; operator handles all logistics
                including restaurant reservations, museum fast-track, and
                inter-country transfers. High-touch concierge service expected.
Must-haves:     Spanish-speaking guide with Egyptology and Jordan expertise;
                private group transport throughout; Nile cruise inclusion;
                Petra full-day; Abu Simbel option; single-operator invoice;
                24/7 WhatsApp support during travel.
Deal-breakers:  Mixed with unknown travelers on coach tours; guide who cannot
                answer substantive cultural questions in Spanish; accommodation
                below 4★; logistics failures (missed transfers, lost bookings);
                hidden costs post-deposit.
