IDENTITY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Demographics:     Ages 35–55; upper-middle to upper class; university-educated
                  professional (lawyer, architect, academic, physician, civil
                  servant); couple or small group of 2–4; based in major metros
                  (São Paulo, Rio, Porto Alegre, Recife); household income
                  $30,000–$80,000 USD equivalent; children grown or none.
Traveler type:    Cultural explorer; intellectual adventurer; long-haul
                  destination collector.
Travel frequency: 1–2 significant international trips per year; has already
                  visited Europe multiple times; seeks "new civilisational
                  chapters" beyond the familiar.

MOTIVATIONS & NEEDS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Primary motivation:    A lifelong fascination with ancient civilisations —
                       Pharaonic Egypt, Mesopotamia, Petra — now acted upon.
                       Egypt represents the fulfilment of a decades-long dream
                       seeded by school textbooks, documentaries, and fiction.
Secondary motivations: Intellectual enrichment; the social prestige of having
                       visited somewhere profound and uncommon; sharing
                       knowledge-rich content (longer captions, reels with
                       historical context) with their network.
Emotional drivers:     Wonder, discovery, intellectual pride, and a desire for
                       authentic connection with something much older than
                       Western civilisation. Also: the romance of the Nile for
                       couples.
Unmet needs:           Expert, historically knowledgeable guides who can hold a
                       sophisticated conversation in Portuguese; itineraries
                       that go beyond the "Big Three" (Pyramids, Luxor, Abu
                       Simbel) to include Saqqara, Karnak at dawn, Siwa Oasis;
                       curated reading lists or pre-trip cultural briefings;
                       quality mid-range accommodation in heritage settings.

DECISION JOURNEY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Trigger:          A National Geographic documentary about the Grand Egyptian
                  Museum opening; a book club reading set in ancient Egypt;
                  a friend who has just returned and can answer detailed
                  questions; a YouTube deep-dive on Egyptian history.
Research phase:   Extended — 3–6 months; reads multiple travel blogs in
                  Portuguese, watches documentaries and vlogs, consults
                  TripAdvisor and Google Reviews for specific guides; compares
                  3–5 operators; scrutinises day-by-day itineraries; asks
                  pointed questions about guide expertise and access to
                  lesser-visited sites.
Comparison:       Experience-first, then price; will pay more for a specialist
                  operator with demonstrably better guides and itineraries;
                  values completeness of Egypt coverage (Upper Egypt mandatory);
                  compares Memphis Tours / Egyptian Sidekick against Kuoni
                  Brazil, G Adventures, and independent custom itineraries.
Decision factors: 1. Guide quality and historical knowledge (in Portuguese)
                  2. Itinerary depth (Upper Egypt, Nile cruise quality)
                  3. Operator reputation and reviews  4. Accommodation quality
                  5. Price — acceptable at mid-to-premium range
Booking channel:  Egyptian Sidekick (Brazilian Site / Egypt) for research
                  entry point; Egypt Tours Plus PT for premium finalisations
                  ($6,317 avg value); Travel Agent referral channel also
                  relevant ($6,015 avg value).
Lead time:        120–180 days; deliberate and unhurried planning cycle.

PRODUCT EXPECTATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Accommodation:  4–5 star boutique or heritage hotels; Nile cruise aboard a
                quality vessel (dahabiya or superior cruise ship); local
                character preferred over international chain anonymity.
Activities:     Pyramids of Giza + Sphinx, Egyptian Museum / Grand Egyptian
                Museum, Saqqara and Memphis, Luxor East and West Banks,
                Valley of the Kings, Karnak and Luxor Temples, Abu Simbel
                (flight), Nile cruise, Alexandria day trip; open to Siwa,
                White Desert, or Nubia village extension.
Duration:       12–16 days; frequently extending into Jordan (Petra, Wadi
                Rum) or Turkey (Istanbul, Ephesus) multi-country tour.
Group model:    Private tour for couple or small group; no shared group tours
                with strangers — privacy and pace control essential.
Service level:  Expert guide, ideally Egyptologist-certified; attentive but
                not intrusive service; flexibility to linger at sites that
                interest them; pre-trip concierge consultation.
Must-haves:     Portuguese-speaking or bilingual Egyptologist guide;
                Upper Egypt coverage (Luxor + Aswan minimum); Nile cruise
                component; well-paced itinerary (not rushed).
Deal-breakers:  Generic, cookie-cutter itinerary; guides with shallow
                knowledge; rushed temple visits; being placed in a large
                mixed-nationality group; English-only documentation.

PRICE & VALUE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Budget range:            $2,000–$5,000 per person (mid to luxury tier);
                         couple total $4,000–$10,000+.
Value perception:        "Depth of experience per dollar." Willing to pay
                         premium for expert guidance, exclusive access, and
                         quality accommodation. Will not compromise on guide
                         quality to save money.
Price anchor:            European cultural tours (Italy, Greece) costing
                         $3,000–$5,000 per person — Egypt must deliver
                         comparable or superior cultural richness at
                         competitive pricing.
Willingness to upgrade:  High willingness to upgrade Nile cruise class
                         (standard → superior → dahabiya); will add Abu Simbel
                         flight, desert extension, or Jordan add-on; readily
                         upgrades accommodation from 4 to 5 star; open to
                         private Egyptologist at added cost.

                      detailed written itineraries; high-quality photography;
                      historical context woven into destination content;
                      testimonials from culturally engaged travellers;
                      TripAdvisor and Google Reviews with substantive detail.
Trusted sources:      National Geographic, Viagem e Turismo magazine (Brazil),
                      specialist travel bloggers and YouTubers with
                      archaeological/historical focus; peer recommendations
                      from educated friends; travel agents with Middle East

Social platforms:     YouTube (deep research), Instagram (inspiration and

                      Portuguese, WhatsApp for agent/operator communication.
Language:             Brazilian Portuguese exclusively for research and booking;

                      expects all commercial communication in Portuguese.
Tone that resonates:  Expert, authoritative, evocative; respects the
                      traveller's intelligence; "you will see what few people
