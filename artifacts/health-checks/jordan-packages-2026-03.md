## 8. Recommendations

### STABLE Classification — Optimize Within Current Structure

**Priority 1: Formalize the dual-product model**
Stop treating Jordan as a single product. Create distinct reporting and strategy tracks:
- **Jordan Add-On** (Italy): Optimize for margin per booking, reduce postponements, streamline operations with DMC. Accept lower value but ensure positive contribution margin. KPIs: attachment rate to Egypt bookings, margin per add-on, postponement rate.
- **Jordan Standalone** (US, Brazil, Germany, others): Optimize for value per booking, invest in content and marketing, develop premium experiences. KPIs: average booking value, solo conversion, lead time.

**Priority 2: Reduce postponement leakage**
The 13.0% postponement rate is the single largest operational drag. Investigate:
- What % of postponements eventually convert to completed bookings?
- Are postponements concentrated in the Italian add-on segment?
- Would deposit structures or early-bird pricing reduce drop-off?

**Priority 3: Protect premium standalone value**
The 2025 average value decline is concerning. Ensure marketing and sales teams are not inadvertently steering premium standalone prospects toward add-on pricing. Consider:
- Minimum pricing for standalone Jordan packages
- Premium inclusions (private guides, luxury camps in Wadi Rum) to justify $5,000+ pricing
- Targeted campaigns to US, Brazil, and Germany markets where willingness to pay is proven

**Priority 4: Solo traveler opportunity**
Jordan's uniquely high solo rate (15.4%) could be a differentiator rather than a drag. Explore:
- Small-group guaranteed-departure Jordan tours targeting solo travelers
- Solo-specific pricing that reflects true per-person operational costs
- Content marketing addressing solo travel to Jordan (safety, logistics, experience)

### What NOT to do
- Do not invest heavily in growing Italian add-on volume — the margin structure may not support it
- Do not merge Jordan performance analysis with Egypt — the dual-product nature requires separate tracking
- Do not treat the 9.6% conversion rate as evidence of product-market fit — it's an artifact of the add-on sales motion, not organic demand
