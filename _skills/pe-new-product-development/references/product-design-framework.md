### Impact-Effort Scoring Guide

| Score | Impact | Effort |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Minimal revenue/audience/brand impact | Negligible — can launch in weeks with existing resources |
| 2 | Small incremental gain | Low — minor new investment or capacity |
| 3 | Moderate meaningful gain | Medium — requires some new resources or 3–6 months |
| 4 | Significant strategic gain | High — major investment or 6–12 months |
| 5 | Transformative — new market or major revenue stream | Very high — multi-year, major ops change |

**Recommended bias**: Favor concepts with Impact ≥ 3 and Effort ≤ 3 (ratio ≥ 1.0) for initial launches.

## § Cannibalization Assessment Methodology

Cannibalization analysis is mandatory — run it in Phase 2 (feasibility), Phase 3 (product design), and Phase 4 (decision record).

### Step 1: Identify At-Risk Products

```sql
-- Intent: Find existing products serving audiences with overlap to this new product
-- Database: system_travelapp via pymysql direct connection

SELECT
  tp.title AS product_name,
  co.name AS source_market,
  ROUND(SUM(aso.selling_rate), 2) AS annual_revenue,
  COUNT(DISTINCT of.id) AS annual_bookings,
  ROUND(AVG(aso.selling_rate), 2) AS avg_booking_value
FROM operation_files of
JOIN requests r ON of.request_id = r.id
JOIN clients cl ON r.company_id = cl.id
JOIN countries co ON cl.country_id = co.id
LEFT JOIN acc_srv_orders_operation_files asof ON asof.operation_file_id = of.id
LEFT JOIN acc_srv_orders aso ON asof.acc_srv_order_id = aso.id AND aso.cancelled = 0
LEFT JOIN tr_packages tp ON r.tr_package_id = tp.id
WHERE of.created >= DATE_SUB(CURDATE(), INTERVAL 12 MONTH)
  AND of.operation_file_status_id NOT IN (5)
  AND co.name IN ([overlapping_source_markets])
GROUP BY tp.title, co.name
ORDER BY annual_revenue DESC;
```
