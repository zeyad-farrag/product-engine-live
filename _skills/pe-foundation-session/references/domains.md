# Foundation Session — Domain Guide

This reference contains the complete exploration guide for all 12 domains. For each domain, adapt your questions based on what you've already learned. These are starting points, not scripts.

---

## Domain 1: Company Identity & Business Model

Understand who the company is at the most fundamental level.

**Explore:**
- Company name, legal structure, founding context
- Core business model — how does the company make money?
- What does the company actually SELL? (Be precise — packages, components, experiences, services?)
- Revenue model — commission, markup, subscription, hybrid?
- What is the company's value chain position? (Tour operator, aggregator, travel agent, DMC, hybrid?)
- Geographic headquarters and operational footprint
- Company size indicators — team size, annual volume, revenue range (if shareable)
- Brand identity — how does the company describe itself to customers? What's the positioning statement?
- What DOESN'T the company do? (Boundaries, exclusions, strategic "no"s)

**Probe deeper:**
- What is the single most important thing a new employee needs to understand about how this business works?
- What do outsiders most commonly get wrong about the business model?

---

## Domain 2: Brand Architecture

Understand every brand the company operates and how they relate.

**Explore:**
- How many brands does the company operate?
- For each brand:
  - Name, positioning, target audience
  - How it differs from other brands in the portfolio
  - URL / primary channel
  - Relative size/importance within the portfolio
- Is there a master brand strategy? (House of brands, branded house, endorsed brands?)
- Are there brands being considered for launch or retirement?
- How do customers discover and differentiate between brands?

**If single-brand:** Explore the sub-segments, product lines, or tiers that function as internal brands.

---

## Domain 3: Destination Portfolio

Understand every destination the company serves and the strategic role each plays.

**Explore:**
- Complete list of destinations currently offered
- For each destination (or destination group):
  - Volume / importance ranking
  - Maturity level (established, growing, emerging, declining)
  - Seasonal patterns
  - Supply strength (own operations, partners, mixed?)
  - Strategic role — is this a hero destination, a diversification play, a cross-sell opportunity?
- Destinations being considered for addition or removal
- Geographic expansion strategy — where next and why?
- Supply chain structure — own ground operations, DMC partners, or mixed?

**Probe deeper:**
- Which destination would hurt the most to lose? Why?
- Which destination has the most untapped potential? What's blocking it?

---

## Domain 4: Source Markets

Understand where the company's customers come from and the strategy for market coverage.

**Explore:**
- Complete list of current source markets (countries/regions where customers originate)
- For each source market:
  - Relative volume / importance
  - Maturity level (established, growing, emerging, untested)
  - Channel mix — how do customers in this market find and book?
  - Language requirements
  - Competitive intensity — how crowded is this market?
- Markets being considered for entry
- Markets that have been evaluated and rejected (and why)
- Is there a market prioritization framework? What criteria drive market entry decisions?

**Probe deeper:**
- What's the difference between a "good" source market and a "bad" one for this business?
- Are there markets where you have demand but choose not to serve? Why?

---

## Domain 5: Customer Segmentation & Tiers

Understand how the company thinks about different customer types.

**Explore:**
- What is the primary segmentation model? (Price tier, travel style, demographics, purpose of travel?)
- For each segment or tier:
  - Name and description
  - Price range / accommodation level
  - Customer profile
  - Volume and margin characteristics
  - Which brands serve which segments?
- Is segmentation consistent across markets, or do different markets have different segment structures?
- How is segmentation enforced in the product catalog? (Separate products, filters, pricing tiers, separate sites?)

**Probe deeper:**
- Is there a segment you'd like to grow? One you'd like to shrink?
- What segment is most profitable? Most operationally complex?

---

## Domain 6: Product Structure & Catalog

Understand what the products actually look like and how they're built.

**Explore:**
- What constitutes a "product" or "package"? (Components, structure, what's included/excluded)
- Product catalog structure — how are products organized? (By destination, by type, by tier, by theme?)
- Catalog size — approximate number of active products/packages
- Product creation process — who builds products, how, and how long does it take?
- Product lifecycle — how do products evolve from launch to retirement?
- Customization model — are packages fixed, semi-flexible, or fully customizable?
- Pricing model — how are products priced? (Per person, tiered, seasonal, dynamic?)
- Content model — what content accompanies each product? (Descriptions, itineraries, images, reviews?)

**Probe deeper:**
- What makes a product "successful" internally? What metrics matter?
- What's the most common reason a product underperforms?
- How do you currently decide to create, modify, or retire a product?

---

## Domain 7: Competitive Landscape

Understand how the company sees its competitive environment.

**Explore:**
- Who are the primary competitors? (List by name)
- For each major competitor:
  - What they offer
  - How they're positioned relative to you
  - Where they're stronger / weaker
  - What you admire about them / what concerns you
- Competitive segments — are there different competitor sets for different destinations, markets, or tiers?
- Indirect competitors — who else competes for the same customer's travel budget?
- Competitive intelligence — how do you currently monitor competitors? (Manually, tools, not at all?)

**Probe deeper:**
- If a customer chooses a competitor over you, what's the most common reason?
- Is there a competitor you'd most like to learn from? Why?

---

## Domain 8: Sales & Distribution Channels

Understand how products reach customers.

**Explore:**
- Direct channels (website, call center, walk-in, email)
- Indirect channels (OTAs, affiliates, travel agents, B2B partnerships)
- Channel mix — what percentage of business comes through each channel?
- Channel strategy — which channels are growing, which are declining?
- Technology stack — booking engine, CRM, analytics tools
- Customer journey — how does a typical customer go from awareness to booking?

**Probe deeper:**
- Which channel has the best conversion rate? The worst?
- Are there channels you'd like to develop but haven't?

---

## Domain 9: Internal Data Landscape

Understand what data exists and how it can be accessed.

**Explore:**
- What systems hold customer and booking data? (CRM, booking engine, databases)
- What database systems are in use? (MySQL, PostgreSQL, etc.)
- What data is available?
  - Booking data (volume, products, dates, source markets, customer segments)
  - Customer data (demographics, contact, booking history)
  - Traffic / web analytics (pageviews, sources, conversions)
  - Amendment data (post-booking changes — what customers modify)
  - Financial data (revenue, margins, costs)
  - Customer feedback (reviews, surveys, complaints)
- Data freshness — how current is the data? Real-time, daily, weekly?
- Data accessibility — APIs, direct database queries, CSV exports, manual reports?
- Known data gaps — what data do you WISH you had but don't?

**Probe deeper:**
- If you could run any query against your data right now, what would you ask?
- What data currently exists but nobody looks at?

---

## Domain 10: Pricing & Commercial Policies

Understand the rules and constraints that govern how products are sold.

**Explore:**
- Pricing structure — per-person, per-room, tiered, seasonal?
- Currency strategy — which currencies do you sell in? How is FX handled?
- Discount / promotion policies
- Cancellation and amendment policies
- Minimum / maximum group sizes
- Lead time requirements — how far in advance must bookings be made?
- Payment terms — deposits, full payment timing
- Any regulatory or compliance constraints by market?

**Probe deeper:**
- What pricing decisions are most contentious or difficult?
- Are there policies that you know hurt conversion but exist for operational reasons?

---

## Domain 11: Strategic Context & Priorities

Understand the current strategic direction and priorities.

**Explore:**
- What are the company's top 3 strategic priorities right now?
- What's the growth strategy? (New markets, new products, deeper penetration, new channels?)
- What's the biggest threat to the business in the next 12 months?
- What's the biggest opportunity?
- Are there any major initiatives underway that this system should be aware of?
- What decisions are coming up that this system could help inform?

**Probe deeper:**
- If you could only achieve one thing this year, what would it be?
- What keeps leadership up at night?

---

## Domain 12: Product Department Context

Understand the team that will use this system.

**Explore:**
- Product Department structure — how many people, what roles?
- Current workflow — how does the team currently do product research, creation, and optimization?
- Tools currently used — spreadsheets, ChatGPT, BI tools, specific software?
- Pain points — what's hardest about the current process?
- What does "good" look like for the Product Department? How is success measured?
- Decision-making process — who approves market entries, product launches, product retirements?
- Collaboration touchpoints — who does the Product Department work with? (Sales, marketing, operations, finance)

**Probe deeper:**
- What would change if the Product Department had unlimited research capacity?
- What question does the team ask most often that nobody can currently answer quickly?
