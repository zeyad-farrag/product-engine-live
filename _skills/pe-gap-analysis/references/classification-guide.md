# Strategic Classification Guide

This file contains detailed classification criteria and action recommendation templates for the Gap Analysis capability. Load this file when determining the strategic classification (Step 5) or generating action recommendations (Step 6).

---

## Classification Decision Framework

### How to Classify

Work through these questions in order. Stop at the first classification that fits.

**1. Is the product fundamentally incompatible with the benchmark?**

If yes → **FUNDAMENTAL_MISMATCH**

Indicators:
- Closing all critical gaps would require changing the product's core purpose or category
- The benchmark expects a product type that this product is not and cannot become
- Even a complete redesign would not close the gap — the starting point is wrong
- Examples: Comparing a budget group tour to a luxury independent traveler expectation; comparing a fixed-itinerary product to a benchmark that demands full flexibility

---

**2. Does the product have multiple critical structural gaps (3 or more)?**

If yes → **REDESIGN_NEEDED**

Indicators:
- 3+ CRITICAL gaps in structural dimensions (product structure, accommodation level, activities, duration/flexibility)
- Closing these gaps requires fundamental changes to what the product *is*, not just how it's *presented*
- Optimization initiatives cannot resolve the gaps — new product development may be required
- The product's current version cannot serve this benchmark without being unrecognizable

---

**3. Are the gaps primarily in how the product is presented rather than what it delivers?**

If yes → **REPOSITIONING_NEEDED**

Indicators:
- 0–1 CRITICAL structural gaps
- Majority of MAJOR gaps are in positioning, content, messaging, or distribution — not product substance
- The product delivers value that the benchmark would appreciate — but the benchmark doesn't know it
- Closing gaps requires a content and messaging overhaul, not product changes
- Examples: A product that has the right inclusions but describes them in the wrong language for the audience; a product positioned as "budget" that actually meets mid-market expectations

---

**4. Does the product have critical or major gaps that are closable with reasonable effort?**

If yes → **FIXABLE_FIT**

Indicators:
- 1–2 CRITICAL gaps or 3–5 MAJOR gaps
- All critical and major gaps are closable — there is a clear path to parity
- Fixing requires effort but does not require rebuilding the product
- The underlying product has the right structure; specific components need upgrading or adding

---

**5. Default: Does the product have no critical gaps and at most 1–2 major gaps?**

If yes → **STRONG_FIT**

Indicators:
- 0 CRITICAL gaps
- 0–2 MAJOR gaps
- The product meets or exceeds benchmark expectations on most dimensions
- Remaining gaps are MODERATE or MINOR — optimization opportunities, not threats
- The product is competitive in this benchmark context

## Classification Profiles

### STRONG_FIT

```
What it means: The product is well-suited to this benchmark. The gaps that exist are refinement 
opportunities, not competitive liabilities.

Strategic implication: 
- Feed findings into a product optimization initiative
- Focus on MODERATE gaps with low effort for quick competitive advantages
- Monitor for benchmark drift — audience expectations and competitive standards evolve

Typical next initiative type: product-optimization
```

**Recommended action structure:**
- Immediate: Address any low-effort MODERATE gaps as quick wins
- Short-term: Close remaining MODERATE gaps for competitive parity
- Medium-term: Address MINOR gaps where ROI is favorable
- Strategic decision: Is this the right benchmark to optimize for, or are there higher-value benchmarks to target?

---

### FIXABLE_FIT

```
What it means: The product has real gaps but a clear path to closing them. The investment is 
justified by the opportunity.

Strategic implication:
- Feed findings into a product optimization initiative with clear milestones
- Priority 1 gaps must be addressed before launching or scaling for this benchmark
- Set a timeline — FIXABLE_FIT with no action plan becomes REDESIGN_NEEDED over time

Typical next initiative type: product-optimization
```

**Recommended action structure:**
- Immediate: Close all CRITICAL gaps before scaling or promoting this product for this benchmark
- Short-term: Close MAJOR gaps; establish competitive parity
- Medium-term: Close MODERATE gaps; move toward STRONG_FIT classification
- Strategic decision: What is the commercial case for making this investment? Is the benchmark audience large enough to justify the fix cost?

---

### REPOSITIONING_NEEDED

```
What it means: The product delivers more value than it communicates. The gap is in presentation, 
not substance. This is often a missed opportunity hiding as a performance problem.

Strategic implication:
- Feed findings into a repositioning initiative
- Do not add product features — fix the communication layer first
- Measure impact: if bookings improve post-repositioning without product changes, the hypothesis is confirmed

Typical next initiative type: repositioning
```

**Recommended action structure:**
- Immediate: Audit and rewrite product descriptions, titles, and positioning for this benchmark's language and priorities
- Short-term: Update images, trust signals, and social proof to speak to the benchmark audience
- Medium-term: Review distribution — is the product visible in the channels this benchmark uses?
- Strategic decision: Is a full repositioning initiative warranted, or can messaging be updated in-place? Who owns the content changes?

---

### REDESIGN_NEEDED

```
What it means: The product's structure cannot serve this benchmark without major surgery. 
Optimization is insufficient — the product needs to be fundamentally different.

Strategic implication:
- Feed findings into a new product development or major restructuring initiative
- Do not invest in incremental optimization of this product for this benchmark — the ROI ceiling is too low
- Consider whether a new product variant is more efficient than redesigning the existing product

Typical next initiative type: new-product-development
```

**Recommended action structure:**
- Immediate: Stop investing in optimizing this product for this benchmark. Redirect resources.
- Short-term: Define what a product designed for this benchmark would look like (new product brief)
- Medium-term: Evaluate build vs. partner vs. acquire options for the new product
- Strategic decision: Is this benchmark important enough to justify new product investment? What is the market size and revenue potential?

---

### FUNDAMENTAL_MISMATCH

```
What it means: The product and the benchmark are the wrong combination. No amount of optimization, 
repositioning, or redesign makes this product right for this benchmark.

Strategic implication:
- Do not invest in closing these gaps. The ROI is negative.
- Use this as a clear input: this product is not for this audience/standard
- Identify what the product IS a fit for and focus there instead

Typical next initiative type: none (rejection decision)
```

**Recommended action structure:**
- Immediate: Remove or deprioritize this product from campaigns targeting this benchmark
- Short-term: Identify which benchmarks this product IS a fit for — run gap analysis against better-matched audiences
- Medium-term: Clarify the product's intended audience in positioning to prevent continued mismatched traffic
- Strategic decision: Is there a commercial reason to pursue this benchmark anyway? If so, it requires a new product, not a modified one. This is a new product development decision.

## Action Recommendation Templates

### Template: Critical Gap Action

```
Gap: [gap name]
Severity: CRITICAL
Current: [current state]
Required: [target state]
Fix approach: [specific change required]
Owner type: [product / marketing / operations / commercial]
Effort: [H/M/L]
Impact: [H/M/L — in terms of purchase conversion]
Dependencies: [other gaps or external factors this depends on]
```

### Template: Positioning Gap Action

```
Gap: [gap name]
Type: MISALIGNED
Current messaging: [what we say now]
Required messaging: [what benchmark audience needs to hear]
Content changes: [specific sections/assets that need updating]
Evidence: [persona card / competitor profile / review data that supports the required message]
Owner type: marketing / content
```

### Template: Strategic Decision Brief

```
Decision required: [clear statement of what needs to be decided]
Context: [the gap(s) driving this decision]
Option A: [first path] — Implications: [cost, time, risk, upside]
Option B: [second path] — Implications: [cost, time, risk, upside]
Data available: [what intelligence exists to inform this decision]
Data missing: [what additional research would reduce uncertainty]
Recommended by analysis: [which option the gap data supports, if deterministic]
Why human judgment is required: [what analytical tools cannot determine — values, risk appetite, strategic priorities]
```

## Classification Consistency Rules

1. **One classification per gap analysis.** A product either fits a benchmark or it doesn't — split verdicts are not valid. If the analysis supports two classifications, pick the more conservative (lower-fit) one and note the ambiguity.

2. **Classification is benchmark-specific.** The same product may be STRONG_FIT for one benchmark and FUNDAMENTAL_MISMATCH for another. Never generalize a classification beyond its specific product-benchmark pair.

3. **Structural gaps outweigh presentation gaps.** A CRITICAL structural gap overrides multiple positive positioning factors. If the product can't deliver what the benchmark requires, no amount of good messaging fixes it.

4. **Excess findings do not improve classification.** Having more than the benchmark needs does not make a FIXABLE_FIT into a STRONG_FIT. Score gaps, not positives.

5. **Classification triggers initiative routing.** STRONG_FIT and FIXABLE_FIT feed product-optimization. REPOSITIONING_NEEDED feeds repositioning. REDESIGN_NEEDED and FUNDAMENTAL_MISMATCH feed new-product-development or rejection. The classification is only valuable if it routes to the right next action.
