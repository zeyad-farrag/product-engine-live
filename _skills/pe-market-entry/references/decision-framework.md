# Decision Framework — Scoring, Strategies, Risk, Criteria

Reference for Phase 2 scoring (Inflection Point 1) and Phase 3 strategy evaluation (Inflection Point 3).

---

## Composite Scoring Rubric (5 Dimensions × 1–10)

Score each dimension 1–10. Calculate composite as unweighted average (or apply weights from foundation context if available).

### Dimension 1: Market Attractiveness (1–10)

Measures the size, growth, and structural quality of the opportunity.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Large outbound market (top 20 globally), strong YoY growth (>8%), high travel spend per capita, favorable structural factors (visa, flights, currency) |
| 7–8 | Mid-large market, moderate growth (4–8%), reasonable structural factors with manageable friction |
| 5–6 | Smaller or slower-growing market, some structural barriers (visa, connectivity gaps), average travel spend |
| 3–4 | Small market, low growth or contracting, significant structural barriers, limited digital maturity |
| 1–2 | Negligible outbound volume, severe structural barriers, high regulatory/political risk, currency instability |

### Dimension 2: Product-Market Fit (1–10)

Measures how well the company's current product portfolio matches what this market's travelers want.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Core destinations strongly align with market's top outbound choices; product types (packages, durations, tier) match personas' stated preferences; internal data shows organic demand |
| 7–8 | Good destination overlap; minor mismatches in product type or price tier that are addressable |
| 5–6 | Partial overlap; meaningful gaps in product mix or destination coverage requiring adaptation |
| 3–4 | Limited overlap; significant product gaps; current portfolio would need substantial changes |
| 1–2 | Near-zero alignment; market wants products/destinations we don't offer |

### Dimension 3: Competitive Opportunity (1–10)

Measures the exploitable white space relative to existing competition.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | No strong incumbents; clear unmet needs identified; market is underserved; competitors have weak NPS / poor reviews |
| 7–8 | Competition present but fragmented or weak in key segments; identifiable differentiation opportunities |
| 5–6 | Competitive market with some gaps; differentiation possible but requires investment |
| 3–4 | Entrenched incumbents with strong brand and distribution; limited white space; price competition dominant |
| 1–2 | Market dominated by one or two large players with deep distribution, high brand loyalty, and no clear gap |

### Dimension 4: Internal Readiness (1–10)

Measures how operationally prepared the company is to serve this market.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Marketing assets in market's language; local payment methods supported; customer service coverage; prior experience with market |
| 7–8 | Most capabilities present; one or two gaps (e.g., language support) that are addressable within 3 months |
| 5–6 | Some capabilities exist; meaningful gaps in language, payment, or support that require 3–6 months to close |
| 3–4 | Significant operational gaps; localization required across multiple dimensions; 6–12 month runway to readiness |
| 1–2 | Essentially starting from scratch; language, payment, regulatory, and support infrastructure all absent |

### Dimension 5: Strategic Alignment (1–10)

Measures fit with the company's stated strategic direction and brand positioning.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Directly supports growth targets; fits brand tier; aligns with leadership priorities; no conflicting initiatives |
| 7–8 | Good strategic fit with minor tensions (e.g., slight tier mismatch); leadership supportive |
| 5–6 | Neutral to strategy; not a priority but not a distraction; limited leadership enthusiasm |
| 3–4 | Marginal fit; entry would stretch resources away from higher-priority initiatives |
| 1–2 | Misaligned; entry would contradict brand positioning, undercut existing markets, or absorb resources from strategic priorities |

### Composite Score Interpretation

| Composite Score | Default Interpretation |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| 8.0–10.0 | Strong ENTER signal; high confidence recommended |
| 6.0–7.9 | Moderate ENTER signal; proceed with clear strategy and resource plan |
| 4.5–5.9 | MONITOR signal; conditions not yet right, define triggers for re-evaluation |
| 3.0–4.4 | Lean REJECT; marginal opportunity, high cost of entry relative to potential |
| 1.0–2.9 | Strong REJECT signal; do not enter |

**Note**: Composite score is a guide, not a rule. A 4.0 with HIGH internal readiness and a strategic pivot context may still warrant ENTER. A 7.0 with critical data gaps may warrant MONITOR. The AI must explain any divergence from the score's default interpretation.

## ENTER / MONITOR / REJECT Criteria

### ENTER
Recommended when ALL of the following hold:
- Composite score ≥ 6.0
- No single dimension below 4/10 (no fatal flaw)
- Key structural barriers are solvable within 6 months
- Data confidence is MEDIUM or higher across at least 3 of 5 domains
- Leadership appetite exists (confirmed in scope discussion)

### MONITOR
Recommended when ANY of the following hold and REJECT is premature:
- Composite score 4.5–5.9
- Strong market attractiveness but unresolved internal readiness gap (would score differently in 6–12 months)
- A key data gap means the score could shift materially with better information
- Market is emerging and current size is small but growth rate is high
- A specific trigger (new flight route, visa change, competitor exit) would substantially change the assessment

**MONITOR is not a cop-out.** Specify:
1. Monitoring horizon (e.g., "revisit in 6 months")
2. Specific trigger conditions that would escalate to ENTER
3. Specific conditions that would confirm REJECT
4. Owner of the monitoring task

### REJECT
Recommended when ANY of the following hold:
- Composite score < 4.5
- A fatal flaw exists: market structurally inaccessible (visa ban, flight gap, currency controls), fundamental product-market misalignment, or strong competitor lock-in with no exploitable gap
- Evidence of a prior failed entry by comparable companies with no structural change since
- Internal readiness score ≤ 2 with no roadmap to close gaps

**REJECT is a success.** A well-evidenced rejection:
- Frees resources for better opportunities
- Creates institutional memory (stored in decision record)
- Defines conditions under which the market should be reconsidered
- Is fully documented with all artifacts (persona cards, competitor benchmarks, assessment report) — none omitted

## Risk Matrix Template

For each entry strategy, rate each risk:

**Risk rating**: Likelihood (H/M/L) × Impact (H/M/L) → Priority (Critical / High / Medium / Low)

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Priority | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|----------|------------|
| Low conversion rate due to brand unknown status | H | H | Critical | Partner with local influencer / OTA for credibility |
| Currency depreciation reducing margins | M | H | High | Price in USD, offer local currency display only |
| Competitor price war response | M | M | Medium | Differentiate on product depth, not price |
| Operational overload on CS team | L | M | Low | Hire local-language CS before launch |
| [add market-specific risks] | | | | |

**Risk priority matrix**:
```
         LOW Impact    MED Impact    HIGH Impact
HIGH Lik  Medium        High          Critical
MED  Lik  Low           Medium        High
LOW  Lik  Low           Low           Medium
```

## MONITOR Trigger Conditions — Example Formats

When recommending MONITOR, specify concrete triggers. Examples:

- "Revisit when direct flights from [city] to [destination] are added by a major carrier"
- "Revisit when market outbound travel volume exceeds [X] million trips/year"
- "Revisit if a major competitor exits this market or significantly reduces presence"
- "Revisit if internal CS team has [language] language support in place"
- "Revisit in 12 months regardless — market is early-stage and conditions may have changed materially"
- "Escalate to ENTER if a local partnership opportunity arises that reduces entry cost to under [X]"
- "Confirm REJECT if [condition] is still true at next review"
